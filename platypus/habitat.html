Habitat

  • The duck-billed platypus are found in the lakes, streams and rivers.
  • They spend most of their time in freshwater ponds and streams
  • They are found in eastern mainland Australia and Tasmania
  • They build their burrows in banks, the entrance is approx 30 cms above the surface of the water.
  • They territories of up to 4.4 miles and the territory of a male will overlap with the territories of 3-4 females.
